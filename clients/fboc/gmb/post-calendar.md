DATE: Day 4 | TYPE: Floral Tip
HEADLINE: Make Your Flowers Last 2x Longer
BODY: You just received a beautiful French Blooms OC arrangement — now let's make 
it last. Here are Jocelyn's top tips for extending the life of fresh-cut flowers: 
First, trim stems at a 45-degree angle every two days to keep water uptake strong. 
Second, change the vase water completely every other day — bacteria is the silent 
killer of fresh flowers. Third, keep arrangements away from direct sunlight, drafts, 
and ripening fruit (ethylene gas from fruit accelerates bloom decay). Fourth, remove 
any petals or leaves that have fallen into the water. Following these four steps 
can easily double the vase life of most arrangements. Have questions about caring 
for a specific bloom? Drop them in the comments or DM us on Instagram — Jocelyn 
loves answering floral care questions.

DATE: Day 14 | TYPE: Floral Tip
HEADLINE: How to Preserve Your Bridal Bouquet Forever
BODY: Your bridal bouquet is one of the most beautiful objects from your wedding 
day — here's how to preserve it. Option 1: Freeze-drying. A professional freeze-dry 
service removes moisture while retaining the shape and color of blooms for 
decades. Results are stunning. Option 2: Hang-drying. Hang your bouquet upside 
down in a cool, dark, dry space for 2–3 weeks. Blooms dry naturally with a 
slightly vintage, romantic look — perfect for a framed display. Option 3: 
Pressing. Individual blooms pressed between heavy books and framed create 
a timeless piece of wall art. Option 4: Silica gel drying. Faster than hang-drying 
with better color retention. Ask Jocelyn at your consultation about her recommended 
preservation partners in Orange County — she'll point you in the right direction 
to protect your blooms forever.

DATE: Day 24 | TYPE: Floral Tip
HEADLINE: Picking Baby Shower Flowers That Last All Day
BODY: Baby showers often run for 4–6 hours, and you want your flowers looking 
fresh from start to finish. Here are Jocelyn's tips for choosing blooms that 
hold up all day. First, avoid flowers that wilt quickly in warm indoor 
environments — gardenias, tulips, and hydrangeas are beautiful but temperature-sensitive. 
Second, do choose flowers that hold their shape in heat: roses, lisianthus, 
spray roses, alstroemeria, and carnations are excellent. Third, keep arrangements 
in cool water right up until setup time — don't assemble and leave them sitting 
for hours before guests arrive. Fourth, mist arrangements lightly with water 
just before the event starts. Fifth, avoid direct sunlight on arrangements during 
the event. Following these five steps ensures your baby shower florals look 
picture-perfect for every photo taken throughout the celebration.

DATE: Day 28 | TYPE: Floral Tip
HEADLINE: How to DIY a Simple Bud Vase Display
BODY: Not every table needs a grand centerpiece — sometimes a collection of 
bud vases is even more impactful. Here's how to create a beautiful bud vase 
display at home. Step one: Gather 7–12 vessels of varying heights — small 
bottles, bud vases, vintage jars, and clear cylinders all work beautifully 
together. Step two: Group them in a loose cluster, taller vases in the back, 
shorter in the front. Step three: Cut single stems short and place 1–3 blooms 
per vessel. Mix textures — a rose here, a spray of eucalyptus there, a single 
lisianthus beside it. Step four: Let some vases hold only a single sprig of 
greenery for breathing room. Step five: Stand back and let the collection feel 
slightly imperfect — that organic quality is the entire point. Want flowers for 
your bud vase collection? We offer cut stems and small bunches for pickup. DM us.
